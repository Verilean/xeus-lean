# Spacetime Algebra & Relativity — Visual Track

A sequel to the [Geometric Algebra](../geometric-algebra/README.md)
track. Everything there — the geometric product, rotors, the sandwich
`v ↦ R v R̃` — carries into relativity by a single change: **the metric
gets a minus sign for time.** Rotations become **Lorentz boosts**,
angles become rapidities, and the same machinery renders how the
universe *looks* to a fast observer, and how a black hole bends the sky.

There is no picture-first, runnable relativity text in the *Visual
Complex Analysis* spirit either; this is a start at one, aimed straight
at the two things people want to *see*: relativistic aberration, and the
distortion around a black hole.

| Chapter | Topic |
|---|---|
| [Ch01 Boosts are rotors](Ch01_Boosts.md) | spacetime algebra `Cl(1,3)`, the light cone, a Lorentz boost as a hyperbolic rotor, relativistic aberration |
| [Ch02 Black holes bend light](Ch02_BlackHole.md) | gravitational lensing, the photon sphere & ring, the shadow, and the lensed image |

## Prerequisites

Read [Geometric Algebra Ch 3 (Rotors)](../geometric-algebra/Ch03_Rotors.md)
and [Ch 7 (3D rotations)](../geometric-algebra/Ch07_3DRotations.md)
first — the boost is *literally* the rotor code with one flipped sign in
the metric.

## How each chapter is shaped

Same as the GA track: a picture (inline SVG), a runnable numerical cell,
and the physics stated plainly. The relativity here is standard GR/SR;
geometric algebra is the language that makes the boost a rotor and (via
Gauge Theory Gravity) the black-hole geodesics a flat-space computation.
